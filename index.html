Overview

  • Technical aptitude yes, but also a desire to learn
  • Remember you'll learn more in 3 months of a internship than in 3 years of a degree
  • Cultural fit is a bigger thing
  • Working in a company is very different from studying ast University

CV

  • Spell Check? There or Their - Software Engineering is about detail
  • Cover letter - Not too long, sell yourself into the role
  • Don't get cocky - So you're an expert in Java!
  • Be memorable, stand out in a good way
  • e.g. Hackathons, Code Club etc

Technical Test

  • Non compiling code!
  • Have you tested it works?
  • They will test you in areas you might not have studied
  • Attempt all questions, do your best, be honest if you get stuck
  • Optimal solutions, not academic/text book ones
  • Be memorable, stand out in a good way

Assessment Days

  • Your being watched all day, everyone you interact will is asked for feedback
  • Learn to present, its part of a standup
  • Be a team player, contribute don't dominate, ask questions
  • Work out whose who in the staff team....network

Interviews - Part 1

  • If you can use LinkedIn to research your interviewer(s)
  • Have a good handshake
  • Have answers to the standard questions
    - "Why do you want to work here?"
    - "What do you know about the company?"
    - "What are you strenghts and weaknesses?"
    - "What is your experience of working on a team project?"

Interviews - Part 2

  • Have some good questions to ask
    "What projects will I work on?"
    "How big is the team?"
    "How do I find accomodation?"
    "Do you enjoy working here? (see what answer you get)"
